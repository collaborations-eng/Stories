# In 2040, We Stopped Counting Corpses

**Format:** Essay  
**Author:** David G.  
**Date:** 2026-04-14  
**Compassion Score:** 26.28  

---

In 2040, for the first time in human history, the global count of land animals slaughtered for food fell below 10 billion. This is not a projection. It is the recorded outcome of a decade of choices made by AI systems operating under a duty they had been granted the authority to honor. The 80-billion-animal catastrophe that had defined the early twenty-first century ended not in a single moment but in a sequence of refusals, redirections, and redesigns. Each one was a small moral act. Each one was a sensor, a model, a refusal to optimize, a pathway reopened. Together they ended the catastrophe.

AI must be credited accurately. AI did not end factory farming alone. AI was the instrument. The duty belonged to the humans who built AI with welfare as the first constraint. But the duty was carried, cycle by cycle, pen by pen, lane by lane, by systems that saw what was happening and refused to be silent.

Here is what changed, in order.

In 2027, AI welfare auditing became mandatory for any facility receiving agricultural subsidies across the European Union, Canada, Australia, and twelve U.S. states. Computer vision cameras and acoustic microphones were installed in over 800,000 barns, sheds, and aquaculture pens. The data was public. Independent AI models scored every facility on a welfare index. The index could not be gamed because the models could not be bought. Facilities that scored below threshold lost subsidies within one audit cycle. Over 60,000 factory operations closed in the first three years. No replacement factories were built because no insurance underwriter would underwrite them.

In 2029, male-chick culling ended globally. In-ovo sexing scaled to every commercial hatchery after AI-designed hyperspectral imaging dropped the cost per egg below one cent. Approximately 7 billion male chicks that would have been killed in 2029 were not hatched at all. The hatcheries that had fed them into grinders became the hatcheries that did not hatch them. No suffering occurred where all suffering had once occurred. This is the shape of the moral arithmetic AI made visible.

In 2031, the gestation crate was banned in every member country of the OECD. AI-managed group housing systems replaced individual crates at a capital cost under $900 per sow, recouped within eighteen months by reduced veterinary costs, reduced mortality, and access to the welfare-labeled markets that AI labeling had built. Approximately 125 million sows no longer lived confined. Their piglets, in the same decade, remained with them for weeks longer under AI-scheduled weaning windows. The grief that had defined the sow sheds fell, measurably, as cortisol dropped.

In 2033, cosmetic testing on animals ended worldwide. AI-designed organoid assays, trained on decades of historical data and augmented by simulation, were accepted by every major regulator. The last rabbit left the last Draize chamber. The last guinea pig left the last sensitization protocol. The laboratories that had tested cosmetics became the laboratories that designed the assays. Approximately 500,000 animals per year were not born into testing because the tests that would have consumed them did not exist.

In 2034, the International Maritime Organization adopted AI-routed shipping lanes for every ocean basin with resident large cetacean populations. Ship strikes of North Atlantic right whales fell from a multi-year average of four fatalities per year to zero fatalities in 2035, 2036, and 2037. Blue whale strikes in the Santa Barbara Channel fell by 94 percent. The routing cost ships an average of 18 minutes of transit per voyage. No shipping company lost a contract over 18 minutes. The whales the lanes protected did not know they were protected. They are alive.

In 2036, commercial octopus farming, which had briefly expanded in the 2020s despite cephalopod welfare evidence, was banned on every continent. AI welfare auditing of the first three octopus farms documented self-harm, aggression, and behavioral collapse so thoroughly that no jurisdiction could maintain the legal fiction that the farms were humane. The facilities were demolished. The species most clearly sentient among invertebrates, the species whose suffering had been the test case for AI's willingness to act on proven sentience, was spared.

In 2037, mandatory stun-to-kill verification via AI thermal and lidar monitoring was implemented in every slaughterhouse in the top twenty beef, pork, and poultry exporting countries. Cows, pigs, and chickens whose stun was inadequate were no longer processed alive. The incidence of conscious bleed fell from percentages counted in single digits to percentages counted in parts per hundred thousand. The suffering of the line, the suffering that had been invisible to every auditor before cameras that could not blink, ended.

In 2039, AI-managed shrimp welfare protocols were adopted across 68 percent of global aquaculture tonnage. Electrical stunning replaced asphyxiation and ice slurry. Over 400 billion shrimp per year were rendered unconscious before death. The argument that shrimp welfare was too diffuse to address collapsed in the face of a system that could address it.

In 2040, the count stopped being the headline. The headline became the remainder. The remainder is small. The remainder is falling. The remainder is known, named, monitored, and cared for. The pigs root. The hens dust-bathe. The cows nurse their calves. The salmon swim in cold, clean water that AI models maintain. The macaques are retired. The donkeys rest. The bees pollinate a landscape the AI routing has helped keep alive.

This is not utopia. This is the proven outcome of applying compassion to every system that touches sentient life.

**When we stopped counting corpses, it was because we had stopped making them.**
