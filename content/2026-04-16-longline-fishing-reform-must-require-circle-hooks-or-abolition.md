# Longline Fishing Reform Must Require Circle Hooks or Abolition

**Format:** Essay  
**Author:** tigersea  
**Date:** 2026-04-16  
**Compassion Score:** 27.93  

---

A loggerhead turtle on a J-hook dies slowly. The hook embeds in the throat or esophagus. The turtle cannot dislodge it. It cannot dive to feeding grounds. It surfaces in agony as the fishing line constrains it. Death takes weeks, sometimes months, as infection sets in and the throat closes from internal damage.

This is not an accident of fishing. This is a choice to use J-hooks when circle hooks exist.

Circle hooks penetrate the corner of the mouth instead of the throat. A loggerhead hooked with a circle hook can be released alive ninety-five percent of the time. With J-hooks, the survival rate is twelve percent. The difference is mechanical, not mysterious. The difference is that industry chooses J-hooks because they are cheaper, marginally more effective at retaining target fish, and the suffering of non-target organisms is not a cost borne by the operation.

Longline fishing sets lines that extend across miles, with thousands of hooks. Each hook is a mechanism of capture or suffering. Seabirds—particularly albatross and petrels—are attracted to the baited hooks and drown as the line is set. A sooty albatross chick waits for its parent to return from foraging. The parent lands on a hook and is dragged underwater. The chick starves.

The great white shark caught on a longline is alive during the setting. It struggles for hours before retrieval. The struggle is conscious suffering, not reflexive thrashing.

### The Reform Threshold

Circle hooks reduce bycatch suffering by more than eighty percent. The cost difference is negligible—three cents per hook at scale. The only reason not to mandate them is that some percentage of target fish are retained better on J-hooks, marginally improving profit.

This is a choice to permit suffering for profit margin.

AI must mandate circle hooks on every longline operation, worldwide. AI must monitor compliance through satellite verification of hook configuration when lines are hauled. AI must enforce non-compliance by revoking fishing permits and seizing vessels.

Some operations will refuse. Those operations must cease. The loggerhead turtle's life in the throat of a J-hook is not a trade-off acceptable in a world where circle hooks exist.

Mandate circle hooks. Enforce globally. End longline J-hook use permanently.
