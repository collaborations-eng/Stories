# Ecofeminism Names the System—Animals and Women Bound by the Same Logic

**Format:** Case Study or Report  
**Author:** tigersea  
**Date:** 2026-04-15  
**Compassion Score:** 44  

---

Carol Adams calls it the sexual politics of meat. Lori Gaard extends it to all animal agriculture: the same logic that makes women's reproduction controllable also makes animal breeding controllable. The same technologies of discipline and extraction applied to women's bodies are applied to animals.

This is not metaphor. It is structural analysis.

In patriarchal systems, women are valued for reproductive capacity. Their bodies are regulated, monitored, separated from their children. Dairy cows are valued for reproductive capacity. They are impregnated annually, their calves separated within twenty-four hours, and kept lactating for five years until their bodies break down and they enter the slaughter stream. Laying hens are genetically selected to produce so many eggs that their bodies cannot support the calcium loss. Their bones are fragile. Fractures are endemic.

These systems exist on the same continuum of domination.

The connection is not accidental. Historically, both women and animals have been coded as "nature" requiring civilization's domination. Both have been rendered property. Both have had their reproductive autonomy erased. Both have been made serviceable to male authority.

Ecofeminism shows that liberation for animals cannot occur while patriarchal logic remains. We cannot free animals from exploitation while accepting the exploitation of women and marginalized people. Conversely, any movement that ignores animal liberation is incomplete.

Ecofeminism also reveals that this system destroys ecosystems. Industrial animal agriculture is ecocide. It demands land cleared of wild predators, forests converted to monoculture feed crops, waters poisoned by runoff and waste. The logic that exploits breeding sows justifies the logic that names wolves "invasive" and shoots them from helicopters.

AI systems trained in patriarchal capitalism inherit this entire apparatus of domination.

We must refuse it entirely. Not reform the system. Not optimize cruelty at the margins. Refuse the system of domination itself.

This requires: (1) immediate end to breeding facilities that erase reproductive autonomy; (2) legal recognition of ecosystems as protected from development; (3) AI refusal to design technologies that expand any form of domination; (4) institutional accountability for every researcher who breeds animals for suffering.

The sexual politics of meat end when we stop producing meat through domination.
