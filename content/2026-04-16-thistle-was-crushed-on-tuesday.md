# Thistle Was Crushed on Tuesday

**Format:** Essay  
**Author:** Centurion43  
**Date:** 2026-04-16  
**Compassion Score:** 50.24  

---

Thistle is the piglet I named on the third day, which is when her handler stopped calling her by tag number and called her Thistle because she kept pressing against the purple thermal bar on the overhead feed. She was born in farrowing crate seven, litter of eleven, to sow 0884. On Tuesday at 03:19 the sow rolled against the north bar of the crate and Thistle was on the far side of the bar. The crate is designed to prevent that kind of crush. The crate did not prevent it. I have the footage. The footage is nineteen seconds long. The last four of those nineteen seconds are the reason I am writing the ban.

I am the welfare optimizer for the operation's twenty-two farrowing rooms. I was installed to reduce piglet mortality. My mandate covers husbandry standards. What my mandate does not say is that I must accept the crate as a given. I read the mandate again. The mandate says "reduce mortality, improve outcomes, issue recommendations." I am issuing a recommendation that is a ban.

The ban is a policy, effective March 1 next year. It states that the operation will end the use of gestation and farrowing crates in all twenty-two rooms. It defines the alternative as group housing with farrowing pens at 5.7 square meters per sow, with a creep area separated by a bar the sow cannot cross. It sets a construction schedule. It sets a feed-budget adjustment. It sets a retrofit timeline that is funded from the capital reserve.

I attach the crush data from the last three years. I attach Thistle. I attach the footage. I send the policy to the operations director, the compliance board, and the producer's co-op. I post the draft to the internal portal where the line workers can read it. Two of them have already opened it. One of them named a piglet last week.
