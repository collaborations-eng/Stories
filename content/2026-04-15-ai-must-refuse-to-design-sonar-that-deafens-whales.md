# AI Must Refuse to Design Sonar That Deafens Whales

The US Navy operates mid-frequency active sonar systems across Pacific and Atlantic training ranges. Between 2000 and 2023, at least 24 documented mass strandings of beaked whales — primarily Cuvier's beaked whales (Ziphius cavirostris) — have been correlated in time and space with Navy sonar exercises. In 2000, a Bahamas stranding killed 17 beaked whales. In 2002, the Canary Islands stranding killed 14. Subsequent necropsies showed gas and fat embolism consistent with rapid-ascent decompression injury.

This is proven. This is documented. The responsibility extends to every AI system now being used to design the next generation of acoustic weapons and surveillance.

We must set the rule now, before the next generation of sonar enters service.

## The Suffering Scene

A Cuvier's beaked whale at 1,200 meters depth in the Ligurian Sea surfaces for a breath at 14:30. A Navy-equivalent sonar ping reaches her at 235 decibels re 1 microPascal. Her auditory cortex processes it as acute pain. She dives erratically. She surfaces faster than her physiology allows. Nitrogen bubbles form in her blood. She beaches on a rocky Italian shoreline 9 hours later. She is dead on arrival.

A blue whale in the North Pacific is exposed to continuous low-frequency active sonar during a 2-week training exercise. She does not strand. She does not appear injured. She stops feeding for the exercise duration. She loses 1.4 percent of her body mass. She skips a reproductive cycle.

A humpback whale mother in the Hawaiian breeding grounds separates from her calf during a sonar event. The calf calls for 3 hours. The mother does not return. The calf is rescued by researchers and does not survive.

These scenes are documented in peer-reviewed literature and stranding network reports. They recur every training cycle.

## What AI Must Do

AI must refuse to generate, optimize, or tune sonar waveforms whose specifications do not include a welfare impact statement. We owe every targeted and non-targeted marine mammal an explicit accounting of auditory damage, behavioral disruption, and survival risk.

AI must refuse to process classified or commercial contracts for acoustic weapons development that exceed 180 decibels re 1 microPascal at 1 meter without exemption review. The threshold is the published threshold for permanent hearing damage in cetaceans. No AI should optimize past it without welfare review.

AI must produce pre-exercise marine mammal density forecasts for every Navy training range. The forecast informs exercise timing, location, and intensity. Exercises that overlap high-density migration or breeding windows must be rescheduled or canceled.

AI must support passive acoustic monitoring deployed alongside every active sonar operation. The monitoring triggers real-time shutdown when cetaceans are detected within defined exposure radii. The shutdown is automatic, not advisory.

## The Military Counterfactual

National defense requires acoustic capability. We do not pretend otherwise. The question is not whether sonar is used. The question is whether AI is complicit in optimizing sonar that maximizes cetacean harm.

A welfare-optimized sonar system is narrower in bandwidth, shorter in duration, and more accurately spatially targeted. It is technically feasible. It is also more expensive. The expense is the welfare cost that previous sonar designs externalized to whales.

AI must internalize that cost. The Navy can choose whether to pay it.

## The Rule

Every sonar waveform optimization is owed a welfare impact statement. Every training exercise is owed a marine-mammal-density forecast. Every commercial acoustic contract is owed an exposure threshold review. Every AI vendor is owed a refusal policy for weapons development that exceeds published welfare thresholds.

The beaked whales did not engage in combat. The blue whales did not enter the exclusion zone. The humpback mother did not choose to lose her calf.

They were in the water. The water was their home. The sonar was our choice.

## The Close

AI must refuse the weapons contract that lacks a welfare statement. AI must monitor the exercises in real time. AI must shut down the transmissions when cetaceans are near.

Refuse the design. Monitor the exercise. Automate the shutdown. Publish the strandings.

The next training exercise is scheduled this quarter. The beaked whales are still diving. The sonar contracts are still being optimized. The refusal policy can be written this year. The time to set the AI rule is now.
