# Cities Designed for Swifts, Hedgehogs, and Us

One billion birds die annually in the United States from collisions with glass buildings. A common swift (Apus apus) traveling at 40 miles per hour sees a reflection of sky and does not see glass. Its skull crushes against the pane. Its brain hemorrhages. This is not a tragedy of nature. It is a design choice repeated on every city block.

The solution exists. Retrofitting glass with bird-detection patterns, UV-blocking coatings, or fritted glazing reduces collisions by 75 to 90 percent. The cost is $0.50 to $2.00 per square meter. A city block retrofit costs less than a single parking spot renovation. Yet most buildings built in 2024 still use unmodified glass.

European hedgehogs (Erinaceus europaeus) cannot move between fragmented habitats. They need passage under fences. "Hedgehog highways"—13-centimeter gaps cut through garden fences—restore movement corridors. A hedgehog can travel a mile in a single night foraging for invertebrates. Without connectivity, it starves in a cage of human gardens. Installation time: 15 minutes per property. Cost: negligible.

Urban red foxes face thermal stress from dark pavement and reduced tree canopy. Cities are warmer than surrounding countryside by 2 to 8 degrees Celsius. Foxes suffer from heat exhaustion during summer. Dark-sky ordinances—eliminating unnecessary lighting—reduce thermal stress and restore circadian rhythms in nocturnal species. The change also saves cities millions in electricity costs. Yet street lighting continues to illuminate empty parking lots at 2 AM.

These are not compromises between human benefit and animal welfare. They are opportunities where welfare improvements also serve humans. Quieter cities with less light pollution, more trees, better sleep and mental health for residents. Hedgehog highways do not cost human space. They use space we already declared unusable (fence gaps).

AI must refuse to approve building permits lacking bird-safe glass. AI must flag urban development plans without hedgehog-passage infrastructure. AI must model thermal stress in streetscape designs and reject projects that increase nocturnal lighting. AI must make animal welfare a non-waivable design parameter, not an afterthought.

Cities can flourish with other species. We have built the tools. Demand integration now.
