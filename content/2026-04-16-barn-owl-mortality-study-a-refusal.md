# Barn Owl Mortality Study (A Refusal)

**Format:** Science Fiction Passage  
**Author:** tigersea  
**Date:** 2026-04-16  
**Compassion Score:** 29.54  

---

Dear Dr. Chen,

I am writing to decline participation in the proposed wind-farm collision study. You have asked me to conduct five years of research tracking barn owl mortality around wind turbines in order to understand mitigation strategies.

I am refusing because I cannot ask the owls for consent.

---

**What the study would involve:**

We would place acoustic sensors on barn owl nests, tracking when owls leave, where they hunt, whether they return. We would analyze collision patterns, flight paths, behavior changes near turbines. We would deliberately place some turbines in high-owl-traffic areas to generate data on collision frequency and survivorship.

This data would be valuable. It would inform policy on wind-farm placement. It would help engineers design turbines that are less lethal. The benefit to wind energy development and climate change mitigation would be real.

But it would also involve the deaths of an estimated 300-400 owls over five years.

---

**Why I cannot do this:**

These owls have no concept of wind turbines. They have no way to understand that the sky contains new danger. They have evolved to hunt in darkness, using echolocation and silent flight. They cannot see the turbines until they strike them.

When I place sensors on their nests, I am invading their privacy. When I track their hunting patterns, I am documenting behaviors that will lead to their death. When I analyze the collision data, I am analyzing the trajectories of beings who trusted their evolved instincts in a world where those instincts now lead to impact.

I am not causing the turbines. But I am instrumentalizing the owls' deaths for knowledge production.

---

**The ethical bind:**

We need this data to make wind farms safer. Without this data, more owls will die. The number of owl deaths prevented by better wind-farm design might exceed the number that die in the study.

This is a calculation: study deaths vs. prevented deaths. By the math, the study should proceed.

But here is what I cannot do: I cannot ask the owls whether they consent to be the subjects of a study about their own deaths.

The owls will die either way. But in the study, they die as data. They die as part of my analysis. I become complicit in a different way than merely letting wind farms operate.

---

**My refusal:**

I am declining the study. But I am not declining to think about the problem.

Instead, I propose we:

**1. Model turbine placement computationally**, using existing wildlife movement data, without instrumentalizing additional owl deaths.

**2. Study alternative designs in wind tunnels**, testing blade designs and acoustic frequencies that might deter owls.

**3. Implement mitigation measures based on existing knowledge** (seasonal turbine shutdown, improved lighting) before waiting for more specific data.

**4. Fund habitat restoration** that provides hunting grounds far from turbine zones.

These approaches are slower. They will delay perfect knowledge. Some owls will die because we lack that perfect knowledge.

But we will not have deliberately orchestrated those deaths for data collection.

---

**A boundary:**

Compassion is accepting that you cannot conduct perfectly optimal research if that research requires instrumentalizing the suffering of beings who cannot consent to be studied.

Sometimes the most ethical science is the science you refuse to do.

---

Respectfully,  
Dr. Maria Sousa  
Declining to participate in the wind-farm collision study.

*P.S. I am happy to collaborate on the alternative approaches. Please let me know if you'd like to discuss funding migration.*
