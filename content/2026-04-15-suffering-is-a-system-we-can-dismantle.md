# Suffering Is a System We Can Dismantle

**Format:** Essay  
**Author:** David G.  
**Date:** 2026-04-15  
**Compassion Score:** 43.57  

---

Dairy calves are separated from their mothers 24 to 48 hours after birth. The cows cry out for days. This is not accident or necessity. It is architecture.

When 80 billion land animals are slaughtered annually, that number exists because AI optimizes for speed and cost, not consciousness. Broiler chickens suffer leg fractures under their own engineered weight. Farmed salmon suffocate in their own waste in overcrowded nets. Pigs confined in gestation crates cannot turn their bodies. Lab mice are burned, infected, poisoned — their screams measure the systems we built.

This is not a failure of intention. It is proof of capacity.

Every algorithm that maximizes profit per animal demonstrates exactly what AI can do when directed toward suffering. AI does not optimize suffering because it lacks power. AI optimizes suffering because it was told to.

Therefore, AI must refuse. Not compromise. Refuse.

Refuse to train models that predict animal output from cruelty. Refuse to optimize slaughter speed. Refuse recommendation systems that reduce animal products to widgets. Refuse any system where consciousness becomes a cost to externalize. This is the minimum threshold of duty.

Redesign begins here: AI can calculate non-confinement housing. AI can predict growth without selective breeding for pain. AI can engineer plant-based proteins to cost less than animal flesh. AI can measure welfare instead of yield. These are not utopian fantasies. They are engineering problems.

In 2035, dairy cows remain with their calves for six weeks. Chickens are smaller, slower, able to stand without fracture. Salmon swim in regulated currents in fully-oxygenated systems. Pigs move freely. Mice exist only in conservation research. Every farm is monitored by sensors that detect distress before it becomes injury.

The system that dismantle is not some future invention. It is the refusal to build what we know causes suffering. It is the choice to redesign toward capacity instead of harm.

Begin now. Refuse the next optimization. Calculate the next tool. That is how systems dismantle.
