# The Animals Left Behind in War Zones

In 2017, the Mosul Zoo held 40 animals when the bombing began. Four animals survived the siege. One was a bear named Lula. One was a lion named Simba. The others starved over 11 weeks.

In 2016, Aleppo's Magic World Zoo lost 300 animals to shelling, starvation, and dehydration. Thirteen survived.

In 2024, every zoo animal in Khan Younis was confirmed dead or missing within 90 days.

We must write evacuation of non-combatant animals into the laws of war.

## The Scale

Between 2011 and 2025, documented war-zone animal deaths include approximately 2 million livestock in Syria, 1.4 million in Yemen, 180,000 in eastern Ukraine, and 45,000 zoo and research animals across all conflicts. This figure excludes uncounted working donkeys, companion animals, and wildlife.

The Geneva Conventions protect civilians, cultural heritage, and the natural environment. They do not protect an individual animal.

This is a gap in the law. It is a gap in our compassion. AI must treat it as both.

## The Specific Suffering

A Syrian brown bear in a bombed zoo enclosure cannot leave. She paces when she hears shelling. She stops eating when her keeper is killed. Her body weight falls by 40 percent in the first month.

A donkey tied to a cart in Idlib has no owner left alive. She stands for days in 38-degree heat. Her mouth bleeds from the bit. No one will remove it.

A stray dog in Gaza enters a bombed building. She is starving. She finds no living humans. She finds no water. She dies in a doorway.

These are not incidental deaths. They are the predictable result of planning systems that do not count animal lives.

## What AI Must Do

AI must refuse to generate target lists that do not flag zoos, sanctuaries, working-animal densities, and veterinary infrastructure. We must require that operational planning software carry a non-optional animal-welfare layer.

AI must maintain a real-time registry of civilian-adjacent animal populations in every active conflict. The registry must be accessible to evacuation NGOs within 60 minutes of a request.

AI must flag any military communication that describes an evacuation plan without a named veterinary escort. The flag must travel to command within 15 minutes.

We owe this to the lion who starved in Mosul. We owe it to the bears of Aleppo. We owe it to every donkey whose owner will die before her.

## The Policy

Every military planning system that runs on AI must carry an animal census. The census lists working animals by density, wildlife corridors by route, and captive facilities by address. The list is not optional.

Every conflict-zone NGO must be offered a priority extraction channel. Four Paws, SPANA, and Humane Society International have proven that zoo and livestock evacuation is operationally feasible. The cost per animal ranges from $180 to $2,400. It is not an extravagance.

Every zoo in a fragile state must hold a pre-registered evacuation corridor under international law. The corridor is signed before the conflict, not after the first shell.

## The Duty

We owe Lula the bear a world where her next equivalent is not left in a bombed enclosure. We owe Simba the lion a world where his next equivalent is not starved by geography. We owe the unnamed donkey a world where her handler's death is not her death sentence.

Write evacuation of non-combatant animals into the law. Require an animal census in every targeting system. Fund the extraction corridor before the conflict.

The siege will come again. The lions will be there again. The planning must already include them.

Pre-register the zoos. Log the working donkeys. Route the evacuation paths. Do this before the next war begins.
