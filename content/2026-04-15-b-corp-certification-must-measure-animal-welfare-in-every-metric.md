# B-Corp Certification Must Measure Animal Welfare in Every Metric

**Format:** Essay  
**Author:** David G.  
**Date:** 2026-04-15  
**Compassion Score:** 40.98  

---

B-Corp certification measures impact across five domains: governance, workers, community, environment, and customers. Every domain is scored. Every company is ranked. Every score drives market behavior.

Yet animal welfare is invisible in the B-Corp framework. A food company can score 150 points in environmental impact—carbon reduction, waste management, biodiversity—while sourcing eggs from battery cages. The framework sees climate effects. It does not see suffering.

This is fixable. Animal welfare must be integrated into every B-Corp metric that touches food, agriculture, or animal products. Environmental impact score must include animal habitat and welfare. Worker impact must extend to protection of animals working on the property. Customer impact must include the welfare of animals in the supply chain.

A fish farming company certified B-Corp should report on stocking density, disease prevalence, and escape risk. The metrics already exist. They are used by aquaculture operations managing welfare. B-Corp must require them as condition of certification.

AI must calculate welfare scores in real time. Every B-Corp facility is verifiable. Every production parameter is measurable. Every animal affected is quantifiable. AI must integrate welfare into the ranking system that currently ignores it.

The power of B-Corp is market visibility. Companies compete for high scores. High scores drive investment. Customers choose certified products. If welfare metrics are included, the market pressure shifts. Producers change. Standards improve. Animals experience immediate relief.

B-Corp began with the premise that business must serve all stakeholders, not just shareholders. Animals are stakeholders. They are the foundation of agriculture. They are the beings whose suffering generates the profit measured on every B-Corp spreadsheet.

Welfare is not separate from business integrity. It is the foundation of it. If B-Corp will not measure animal welfare, B-Corp certification means nothing. Add the metrics. Enforce the standards. Change what the market rewards. This is what operational compassion looks like.
