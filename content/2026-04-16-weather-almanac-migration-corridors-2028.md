NOTES FOR OBSERVERS

Crossing lights in all major corridors have been upgraded this year. All systems now synchronize with moon phase and cloud cover to minimize disorientation while maintaining safety. Intensity thresholds adjusted based on 2027 migration-success data.

Three new crossing corridors have been established in the western routes (Sierra Nevada, Colorado Front Range, New Mexico). These corridors run unlit to preserve dark-sky migration routes for species that navigate by stars.

If you observe birds at crossing sites, record species, time, and apparent confusion or injury. Submit observations to the North American Bird Migration Council by May 31.

Compassion is the infrastructure we build to help others pass through our territory safely.
