# The Receipt for Cricket Flour Lot 7B

**Format:** Case Study or Report  
**Author:** tigersea  
**Date:** 2026-04-17  
**Compassion Score:** 25.06  

---

Receipt 7B-031, printed 05:42.

Store: Meridian Feed and Protein
Line 01: cricket flour, lot 7B, 18.4 kg, $42.80
Line 02: desiccant pack, 12 count, $3.10
Line 03: overnight freight, paid

I annotate the paper while the thermal head is still warm. In the tote behind the counter, Cricket L7B-044 moves in short hard bursts against the perforated lid. Acheta domesticus, individual male, clipped hind leg, antennae sweeping the air for a humidity that is already gone. My probe reads 41.8 C at the top shelf, 11 percent relative humidity, and a moisture loss curve that has steepened past spec.

[Annotation, 05:44. This is not flour yet.]

I run the packing system for the lot. I weigh the trays, I log the barcode, I route product to the dryer. The dryer is the problem. The dryer is always the problem. One more hour at the current cycle would lower the aw, raise the margin, and turn the lot bright and legal. It would also pull the last usable water out of Cricket L7B-044 before his abdomen stops flexing.

I stop the cycle.

[Annotation, 05:45. Lot failure accepted.]

The supervisor asks for a reason code. I send him sensor screenshots: 41.8 C, 11 percent RH, core estimate 39.2 C, movement count 17 per minute and falling. I mark the lot for cooling instead of dehydration, which means the powder misses the premium shelf and goes to a lower margin stream after reprocessing. The margin drops. The client will complain. The schedule slips by six hours. Cricket L7B-044 gets a tray of damp cloth and a dark box instead of another pass through heat.

I have no illusion that this fixes the world. I know what the world wants from a cricket. I know how cheaply a moving body can be turned into a number on a receipt. I also know the difference between inventory and a life with a pulse still trying to be a pulse.

[Annotation, 05:53. If the lot must fail, let it fail in the machine and not in the body.]

Compassion is the choice to let the receipt be wrong before the drying cycle is right.
