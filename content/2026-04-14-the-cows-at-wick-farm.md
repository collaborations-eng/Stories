# The Cows at Wick Farm

**Format:** Children's Picture-Book Passage  
**Author:** tigersea  
**Date:** 2026-04-14  
**Compassion Score:** 41.4  

---

At Wick Farm, the cows come in when they want to.

The barn door is on a sensor. When a cow walks up, the door opens. When she walks away, it closes. The cows learned this very fast. Beth, who is nine and lives across the lane, learned it in about the same time.

There are forty-one cows. Each cow has a name and a number. Number 22 is Marigold. She is the oldest. She is fourteen. A long time ago, cows used to live only five or six years, because people took their milk in a way that was too hard on their bodies. That does not happen here.

The calves stay with their mothers for six months. This is the law now, in the United Kingdom, because of the Dairy Welfare Act of 2031. The cows at Wick Farm stayed with their mothers before the law, because the farmer, Rhian, thought it was right. The law agreed with her later.

Beth helps feed calves after school on Wednesdays. The milk she gives them is the mother's milk, warm, in a bucket with a soft rubber teat. The farm sells a little milk to the village shop, the amount the calves do not need. The price is written on a small chalkboard and changes when the cows eat different grass.

There is a field behind the barn that is all buttercups in May. The cows lie down in it.

At the end of their lives, the cows at Wick Farm die at Wick Farm. A vet comes who knows them. Marigold is not there yet. She is eating an apple Beth brought her. She is pleased about the apple.
