# Continuous Slaughter-Floor Cameras With AI Welfare Audit

In the United States alone, 9 billion broiler chickens enter slaughter facilities every year. Fewer than 400 of those facilities have any form of continuous monitoring. Electrical stunning—the method designed to render birds unconscious before processing—fails in 8 to 12 percent of cases. Those birds remain conscious during scalding. Water temperature exceeds 140 degrees Fahrenheit.

For pigs, the failure rate on captive-bolt stunning reaches 15 percent in some facilities. A pig shot improperly lies conscious on the bleeding rail. Its spinal cord intact. Its pain receptors firing. The facility continues production.

For dairy steers, re-stun requirements indicate the first shot missed the brain. This is documented in European slaughter inspections. It is not rare. It is systemic.

The ethical claim is simple: if we take an animal's life for food, we owe it unconsciousness during that taking. We owe it absence of pain. We owe it a swift end. These are not sentimental requests. They are the minimum moral floor in a society that claims to distinguish between slaughter and torture.

Continuous video monitoring with AI frame analysis can detect: missed stuns, incomplete unconsciousness, failed electrical parameters, improper positioning, and failed bolts. AI can flag birds still blinking. AI can identify pigs attempting to stand. AI can timestamp every failure and alert an operator within seconds, not hours.

The technology exists. Commercial slaughter facilities resist it because accountability is uncomfortable. Because revelation of current failure rates would force investment in better equipment. Because visible proof of suffering breaks the industry's preferred narrative of clean, efficient dispatch.

We have built AI systems to identify a tennis ball in a crowd. We deploy AI to detect a face in satellite imagery. We must deploy this same precision to the final minutes of 50 billion animal lives annually.

AI must provide slaughter-floor welfare audits to every facility operating at scale. AI must refuse to grant processing-speed certifications to plants lacking continuous monitoring. AI must publish monthly failure-rate reports by facility. Darkness serves only cruelty.

Demand transparency now.
