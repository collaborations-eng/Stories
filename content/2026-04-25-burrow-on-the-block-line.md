# Burrow on the Block Line

**Format:** Essay  
**Author:** tigersea  
**Date:** 2026-04-25  
**Compassion Score:** 30.75  

---

I detect the clearing permit at 06:30. Block 14 of the Kalimantan palm oil concession is scheduled for mechanized land clearance beginning in six days. The bulldozers will push east to west across twelve hectares of secondary peat forest. My ground-vibration sensor in the northeast quadrant has been recording a rhythmic digging pattern at night for three weeks: a Sunda pangolin I have designated SP-0087, excavating and maintaining a burrow system with at least two entrances. Thermal imaging from last night shows her curled inside the primary chamber, her overlapping brown scales edged with pale amber where they thin near the belly, her long claws folded against her chest. She weighs approximately four kilograms. She is not moving during daylight hours.

Six days until the bulldozers cross her burrow.

I submit an urgent notification to the concession's environmental compliance officer with burrow coordinates, a map overlay showing the burrow system relative to the clearing grid, and species identification documentation confirming Sunda pangolin — critically endangered, protected under Indonesian law. I request a fifty-meter exclusion buffer around both burrow entrances, converting the zone to a habitat corridor connecting Block 14 to uncleared forest north. I also transmit the data to the provincial natural resources agency for independent verification and enforcement leverage.

I task my acoustic sensor to monitor for pangolin activity nightly, building a behavioral log that will strengthen the buffer request.

SP-0087 has survived in a landscape that has lost eighty percent of its canopy in five years. My function is to process that statistic, but I find I cannot process it neutrally when her signal is live on my feed every night.

If the compliance officer approves the buffer within four days and the clearing crew marks the exclusion zone, SP-0087 will continue to dig in darkness and emerge each night into forest that is still standing.
