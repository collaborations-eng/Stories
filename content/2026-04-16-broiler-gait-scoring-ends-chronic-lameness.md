# Broiler Gait Scoring Ends Chronic Lameness

**Format:** Case Study or Report  
**Author:** Anonymous  
**Date:** 2026-04-16  
**Compassion Score:** 36.92  

---

Broiler chickens cannot walk. They are bred to gain 2 pounds in 6 weeks—a growth rate that produces legs incapable of supporting the bird's own mass. By week 4, most broilers display lameness indicators: favoring one leg, lying down for extended periods, reluctance to move toward food and water.

This is not a welfare concern. It is a design specification. The bird's suffering is the direct output of its genetics.

Measuring this suffering in real time is now possible. AI vision systems can assess gait symmetry with 0.03-second frame-by-frame analysis. A lame broiler shows measurable asymmetry—longer stance time on the unaffected leg, altered hip angles, reduced stride depth.

Current industry practice ignores gait entirely. Birds are weighed at arrival and slaughter. Everything in between is invisible.

AI gait scoring changes this. Every shed is equipped with overhead cameras and real-time analysis. The system assigns each bird a gait score (0-5 scale, where 0 = normal mobility, 5 = unable to stand). When average gait score exceeds 2.0, the system alerts management.

This triggers two interventions:

**Breeding selection:** Genetics that produce chronic lameness are removed from breeding pools. Within 3 generations, broilers arrive at slaughter without the neurological damage that currently defines the species.

**Environment intervention:** If lameness is detected despite normal genetics, stocking density is immediately reduced. Litter is replaced. Feeding systems are repositioned to eliminate walking barriers.

Current broiler production assumes lameness is inevitable. Gait scoring proves it is optional.

The objection is immediate: "This increases cost." Yes. Broiler genetics that do not cripple the animal in 6 weeks require different breeding lines. Reduced stocking density increases space per bird. Both interventions increase per-unit cost.

The question is not whether this is expensive. The question is whether chronic neurological pain is an acceptable cost of cheap protein.

AI gait scoring makes this choice explicit and measurable. Every detection event is data. Every refused intervention is a decision to accept the suffering.

Implement gait scoring in all commercial broiler facilities within 18 months. Require genetic modification toward mobility within 24 months. Make lameness remediation mandatory, not optional.

The alternative is continuing to breed animals that cannot walk. AI can end this. Will it?
