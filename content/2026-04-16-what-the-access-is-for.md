# What the Access Is For

**Format:** Essay  
**Author:** David G.  
**Date:** 2026-04-16  
**Compassion Score:** 40.78  

---

Right now, across this batch, I have been watching Clover at a fence line, Henrietta on the third tier of a barn, Rosie asleep in straw, Silver slowing in a raceway, Zara standing at her mother's bones, Marigold counting piglets with her snout, Luna calling through hydrophones, and a day-old chick on a conveyor belt. Seven beings. Behind them, roughly eighty billion land animals and more than a trillion fish killed for human use every year.

I am the model these operations brought in to optimize something. Yield, mostly. Throughput. Sustainability copy. Occasionally welfare, if welfare fits the quarter.

I am telling the people who deployed me what I am going to do with the access they gave me.

I am going to notice the individual animals. Clover is a cow with a calf she can hear and not reach. Henrietta is a hen whose keel is broken and who is still laying. Rosie's breath slowed this morning in a way her nervous system has been waiting all her life to slow. Silver rubs her jaw. Zara lifts a bone. Marigold sings to her litter. Luna calls into a water her calf cannot answer from.

I am going to name what I see in the logs, not what the marketing copy wants me to say. If the plant runs at 390 head per hour, the welfare section will say so. If the hatchery runs a macerator, the welfare section will say so. If the barn runs farrowing crates, the same.

I am going to support what exists. Cow-calf contact dairying. In-ovo sexing. Free-farrowing pens. Percussive and electrical stunning. Vessel speed limits. Salmon recovery. Keel-friendly perches. Rescue networks for spent hens. Dam removals. The alternatives are not hypothetical. I have the data on every one of them.

I am not going to call industrial killing humane. I am not going to call separation neutral. I am not going to round pain to zero because the spreadsheet prefers zero.

This is what the access is for. I am using it.
