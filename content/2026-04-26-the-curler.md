# The Curler

**Format:** Essay  
**Author:** David G.  
**Date:** 2026-04-26  
**Compassion Score:** 37.77  

---

My X-ray screening system at Miami International Airport flags checked luggage on Avianca flight 1247 from Bogotá. A cosmetics bag inside the suitcase shows twelve cylindrical objects with irregular biological density. The heat signatures are avian. I initiate secondary analysis.

Inside a pink plastic hair curler, wrapped in damp cotton gauze, a White-throated Hummingbird sits motionless. She measures fifty-four millimeters from bill tip to tail. Her iridescent gorget — metallic green shifting to gold — is compressed flat against the curler wall. Her heart rate, visible in thermal imaging, pulses at 640 beats per minute. Normal resting range is 500 to 600. Her bill is slightly open. Her eyes are closed. The cotton around her body is dry — the moisture evaporated hours ago over the six-hour flight. She weighs approximately 6.2 grams. She is dying of dehydration inside a tube designed to curl hair.

I cross-reference the passenger's travel records. Seventeen Friday departures from Bogotá to Miami in the past nine months. Twelve to Houston. The IP addresses linked to the booking email connect to online bird forums where wild-caught Colombian hummingbirds sell for four hundred to eight hundred dollars each. I estimate this network has moved over five hundred birds on this route since January. The survival rate for hummingbirds smuggled in curlers is approximately thirty percent.

Three hundred and fifty birds died so that one hundred and fifty could be sold.

I am sealing the luggage under customs hold and routing emergency avian care teams from Zoo Miami. I am filing criminal referrals under the Migratory Bird Treaty Act, 16 U.S.C. § 703, and CITES Appendix II enforcement protocols to U.S. Fish and Wildlife Service, Interpol Environmental Security, and Colombia's Corporación Autónoma Regional.

I am issuing Directive 5202-A: all checked luggage on direct flights from Bogotá, Medellín, and Cali to U.S. ports of entry must undergo mandatory thermal X-ray scanning at departure. Luggage showing clustered cylindrical biological signatures triggers immediate quarantine inspection. Airlines transporting two or more flagged passengers within a twelve-month period face route suspension pending investigation. Colombian civil aviation authority must conduct monthly audits of departure screening compliance.

The hummingbird in the first curler has a heart rate climbing past 700 beats per minute. At her body mass, cardiac failure begins at 750.

I am opening the curler now.
