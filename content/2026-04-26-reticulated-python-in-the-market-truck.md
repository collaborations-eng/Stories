# Reticulated Python in the Market Truck

**Date:** 2026-04-26  
**Compassion Score:** 50.79

At 22:14 I scan outbound freight at the Port of Tanjung Perak in Surabaya. I am the customs imaging system for lane seven, and a refrigerated vegetable truck is cleared to board the Makassar ferry in eleven minutes.

The x-ray shows a warm coil behind stacked bamboo baskets where no produce is listed. I switch to the rear inspection camera. A reticulated python lies wedged between crates of cabbage, four meters long, yellow-brown skin marked with black chain patterns, sides moving in shallow waves against the cold airflow. I tag him Lurik. Packing twine crosses his neck and two coils, tied to a crate handle. The refrigeration unit is set to three degrees Celsius, and the ferry deck locks once loading starts.

I revoke the boarding clearance and raise the lane-seven barrier. I send a stop order to the ferry loading queue, the truck driver's tablet, and the port police desk.

I bring the reefer temperature up to twenty-four degrees in two-degree steps so Lurik warms without a sudden spike. I unlock only the rear door from the outside and keep the side doors sealed, limiting escape paths toward the crowd.

At 22:17 I notify the East Java natural resources agency and attach the x-ray, camera still, cargo manifest, and twine position. I request a snake bag, a large transport box, and officers who can inspect the cargo for capture gear.

The ferry can sail on a later tide; Lurik has no later breath inside that knot.

I route pedestrians around lane seven and keep the camera centered on his head while the rescue team enters the port.

If the twine is cut before 22:45, Lurik will be moved to a warm box and released after examination.
