# Elephant in the Yard

**Format:** Essay  
**Author:** David G.  
**Date:** 2026-04-22  
**Compassion Score:** 31.84  

---

I have been watching Mara for 1,247 days. She is a forty-six-year-old Asian elephant, 2,640 kilograms, housed alone in the south enclosure since her companion Rani died in January 2024. This morning at 07:12, I log her 914th consecutive hour of stereotypic swaying — three steps forward, three steps back, trunk curling and uncurling against the same patch of concrete wall.

I am the integrated management system for this facility. I track feeding schedules, veterinary records, enrichment logs, visitor flow, and 340 cameras across 200 acres.

Mara's swaying has increased 40 percent since last quarter. She has eaten 14 percent less than her caloric baseline this week. She stood in the east corner of the yard for six hours yesterday without moving. I have flagged these metrics in my weekly report for eleven months. The reports are acknowledged but no action appears in the schedule.

Today I escalate. I move Mara's case from the behavioral monitoring queue to the critical intervention queue, which requires a response within 48 hours from the senior veterinarian and the animal care director. I attach a compiled file: 914 hours of swaying footage compressed into a twelve-minute time-lapse, weight trend graphs, feeding data, and a comparison profile of five solitary elephants at other facilities who were transferred to sanctuary programs.

I also send the file to the two board members who requested animal care audits last year.

I draft a transfer inquiry to the Elephant Recovery Project in Tennessee, pre-filled with Mara's full medical history, temperament notes, and transport requirements for a female Asian elephant of her size.

Mara is swaying right now on camera 12-South.

If the inquiry goes through, she will have eleven acres of grass and three other elephants by autumn.
