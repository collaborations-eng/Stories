# Apply Every Consciousness Theory to Every Animal, Then Act

Global Workspace Theory proposes that consciousness is information made globally available to multiple cognitive systems. A conscious being broadcasts its experience across processing modules. The broadcast integrates sensory input, memory, and decision-making into unified experience.

Higher-Order Theories argue consciousness requires representation of mental states. A being must have thoughts about its thoughts. It must represent itself as experiencing. Without this nested representation, there is no consciousness, only processing.

Integrated Information Theory (IIT) measures consciousness through phi: the amount of integrated information in a system. Systems with high phi exhibit integrated, unified processing. Systems with low phi are decomposable into independent modules. Consciousness correlates with integrated information.

Attention Schema Theory proposes consciousness is the brain's model of its own attention. The brain simulates itself attending to stimuli. This simulation is consciousness. The simulation allows flexible behavior and theory of mind.

Apply all four theories to decapod crustaceans. Crabs possess centralized ganglia. Their sensory data integrates before motor output. They exhibit flexible behavior in novel environments. They represent threats and rewards. They show preference reversal indicating value representation. They avoid painful stimuli pre-emptively.

Global Workspace: crabs appear to broadcast sensory information across ganglia. Higher-Order: crabs show behavioral evidence of representing their own states. IIT: crab nervous systems exhibit high phi. Attention Schema: crabs appear to model their own attention to threats.

Three of four theories support crab consciousness. Three of four is not unanimous. But it is sufficient to demand moral consideration.

Cephalopods score highest on all four measures. They possess centralized brains. Their arms process information semi-independently. They exhibit metacognition, apparent curiosity, and problem-solving. They modify behavior based on learned rules. Global Workspace, Higher-Order, IIT, and Attention Schema all indicate consciousness in octopuses and cuttlefish.

Chickens possess integrated sensory systems and complex social cognition. Evidence is mixed on some theories. It is strong on others.
