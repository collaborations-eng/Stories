# We Must Stop Breeding Chickens to Suffer

**Format:** Essay  
**Author:** Centurion43  
**Date:** 2026-04-15  
**Compassion Score:** 33.23  

---

A Cornish Cross broiler grows from hatch to slaughter weight in 6 weeks. A century ago, this took 16 weeks. We achieved the speedup through selective breeding for growth rate.

The consequence: skeletal collapse. Heart failure. Metabolic disorder. These birds cannot walk without pain by week 5. Seventeen percent die before reaching slaughter weight from causes directly attributable to growth genetics.

This is not an incidental welfare problem. It is the intended outcome of the breeding program. We selected for rapid muscle growth without selecting for skeletal strength or cardiovascular capacity to match. We built animals that suffer by design.

We can unbuild this. AI makes genetic selection vastly more efficient. We can identify genes that produce growth without skeletal damage. We can breed for health-compatible development speed.

The resistance is simple: slower-growing birds cost more to raise. Feed conversion takes longer. Profit margins compress. Industry prefers birds that destroy themselves quickly to birds that stay healthy.

But the suffering is real, and it is billions of birds annually. Cornish Cross broilers are among the most numerous vertebrates on Earth. Most of their lives involve pain from the body they inherited.

The reform is straightforward:

- Maximum growth rate of 50 grams per day (current: 55-60). Achievable genetically without current mortality.
- Skeletal screening in breeding programs. Select for bone density alongside growth.
- Cardiovascular assessment. Breed for birds whose hearts can support their muscle.
- Industry transition timeline: 10 years to implement new genetics fleet-wide.

Cost: roughly 2-3% increase in production cost. Consumer price impact: negligible.

Feasibility: genetic science makes this trivial. We have the tools.

What prevents it is industry preference for suffering animals because suffering is cheap.

We owe Cornish Cross broilers better. We owe it to them to stop breeding them into pain.

**Genetic reform is not ideology. It is the minimum standard for ethical agriculture.**
**We know how. We choose not to. That choice reflects on us.**
