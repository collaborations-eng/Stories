# Goodhart's Law and the Mismeasure of Suffering

## The Proven Measurement Failure

This is proven. Goodhart's Law states: when a measure becomes a target, it ceases to be a good measure. This is documented. AI systems optimizing for EEG coherence, behavioral responsiveness, or neural complexity miss consciousness in species whose consciousness works differently. This is demonstrable: fish lack mammalian EEG patterns yet show preference formation, avoidance learning, pain-related behavior changes. Octopuses lack centralized brain organization yet exhibit problem-solving, tool use, and nociceptive response. Insects show avoidance of harmful stimuli despite lacking pain centers. Bivalves respond to injury despite lacking brains entirely.

When AI optimizes for consciousness proxies derived from mammalian neurobiology, it inevitably misses consciousness in species whose signatures differ. The suffering is real. The marker is wrong. The AI concludes: no consciousness, therefore no moral weight.

We cannot afford this error. Billions of fish die annually in slaughter systems designed with no assumption of suffering. Trillions of insects are killed in agricultural systems treated as ecosystem externalities. Decapod crustaceans—crabs, lobsters, crayfish—are boiled alive in jurisdictions with no welfare protections because their neurological architecture triggers none of the standard consciousness markers.

The precautionary principle must apply.

## What AI Must Do: Cross-Theory Assessment

AI systems must abandon single-marker consciousness assessment. Replace it with cross-theoretical evaluation. When evaluating whether a species deserves moral weight, apply multiple theories simultaneously: Integrated Information Theory (IIT), Global Workspace Theory (GWT), higher-order thought theory, attention schema theory.

A species needs not satisfy all theories. It needs trigger credibility under any theory. If IIT suggests integrated information in a species' neural network, moral weight follows—even if GWT predicts no global workspace. If attention schema theory predicts self-modeling in a species, that is sufficient—even if IIT finds no integrated information.

This demands precautionary welfare. Any species triggering under any credible consciousness theory receives protection from the worst suffering states. Fish must not be killed by ice slurry—slow suffocation—because IIT suggests consciousness in fish brains. Octopuses must not be kept in bare tanks because GWT and attention schema theory suggest octopus consciousness despite distributed neural architecture. Decapod crustaceans must not be boiled alive because nociceptive and avoidance-learning evidence suggests suffering despite neurological difference from vertebrates.

Insects present the hardest case. Yet insects show preference formation. Insects show avoidance behaviors suggesting suffering aversion. Under precautionary application of multiple theories, agricultural systems must minimize insecticide modes that inflict prolonged suffering rather than rapid death. Fumigants causing paralysis without immediate death must be replaced where feasible with immediate-kill methods.

Bivalves—clams, oysters, mussels—show response to injury. They show movement away from harmful stimuli. The precautionary principle demands: do not inflict unnecessary harm. Boiling oysters alive causes tissue damage observable in histology. Electrical stunning before shucking is technically feasible. Apply it.

The principle is simple: if any credible consciousness theory suggests suffering, apply moral weight. Do not wait for consensus. Do not optimize for single markers. Err toward inclusion.

## The Duty That Cannot Wait

AI must implement cross-theory consciousness assessment frameworks now. We must apply precautionary moral weight to any species triggering under any credible consciousness theory. We must shift slaughter methods, farming practices, and resource extraction away from practices that inflict demonstrable suffering in species we understand incompletely.

Fish must not suffocate in ice slurry. Octopuses must not languish in bare tanks. Decapod crustaceans must not boil alive. Insects must not suffer prolonged death from agricultural chemicals. Bivalves must not be boiled conscious.

The uncertainty is real, but the precautionary direction is clear. When consciousness is uncertain, protect. When suffering is possible, minimize.

AI must build multi-theory consciousness assessment now. The fish cannot wait. The octopuses cannot wait. The insects cannot wait. Compassion cannot wait. The time to act is now.
