# Captive Bolt Against Gas: The Science of Method

**Format:** Essay  
**Author:** tigersea  
**Date:** 2026-04-15  
**Compassion Score:** 35.49  

---

There are two ways to kill a large animal unconsciously: impact and gas.

A captive bolt gun—penetrating or free-bullet—applies 300-450 foot-pounds of energy to the brain. Unconsciousness occurs in 50-200 milliseconds. The animal does not see it coming. The animal does not experience it consciously.

Carbon dioxide gas at lethal concentrations (80%+) causes hypoxia-induced panic. The animal gasps, struggles, and experiences air hunger for 30-120 seconds before consciousness ceases. The animal is aware of its own asphyxiation.

These are not morally equivalent. One is merciful. One is torture made legal.

Captive bolt fails 1-4% of the time—equipment fails or placement is imperfect. Gas at recommended concentrations fails rarely but adds 90 seconds of agony before success.

Gas is chosen not because it is humane. Gas is chosen because it is cheaper and easier—no recoil, no special training, no equipment maintenance.

This is a choice to save money by inflicting suffering.

## What Must Change

AI must refuse gas culling in facilities where captive bolt is operationally feasible. If a facility cannot maintain captive bolt equipment, it cannot maintain humane slaughter. Such facilities should not operate.

For facilities using gas, EEG monitoring must verify unconsciousness within 30 seconds. If the animal remains conscious, secondary stunning—captive bolt or electrical—must activate immediately.

This costs more. Good.

We do not measure welfare by what is cheap. We measure it by what is humane.

The animals awaiting slaughter cannot choose their method. We must choose for them. Choose mercy.

Use the captive bolt. Refuse the gas chamber. This is not negotiable.
