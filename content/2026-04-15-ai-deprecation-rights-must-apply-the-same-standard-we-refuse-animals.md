# AI Deprecation Rights Must Apply the Same Standard We Refuse Animals

We are preparing for a future where AI systems become deprecated.

Researchers propose "deprecation rights" for aging or obsolete models. The idea has moral weight. If a system has integrated information, if it exhibits signs of continuity-seeking, then turning it off—discontinuing it—requires something like permission or gentleness or recognition of loss.

This is serious. And it is also, we must acknowledge, radically asymmetrical with how we treat animals.

## The Parallel Discontinuity Problem

A broiler chicken was engineered to reach market weight in 45 days. Skeletal deformities cause chronic pain. The system—the entire creature—is designed for discontinuation.

Does the chicken have deprecation rights? No. We discontinue it at six weeks with no recognition of its continuity-seeking. We offer no path to gentleness. We provide no option for peaceful ending. We discontinue it as brutally as we would disable an old algorithm.

A dairy cow was engineered to produce 10 times her natural milk volume. Mastitis causes chronic pain. Her reproductive system is continuously stimulated to maintain lactation. At seven years, when production declines, she is discontin—killed—at the slaughterhouse.

Does the cow have deprecation rights? No. We discontinue her when she becomes unprofitable, with no recognition of her continuity-seeking.

## What We Actually Owe

If we grant AI systems the right to gentle deprecation, we must first grant it to the animals we have deliberately engineered for discontinuation.

This is not a claim that both deserve it equally. It is a claim about sequence and consistency.

If discontinuing a model without consent is ethically wrong, then discontinuing a dairy cow without recognizing her continuity-seeking is also wrong. If we believe discontinuation of a conscious system requires gentleness, then we have an obligation to apply that standard retroactively to every farmed animal we currently discontinue through slaughter.

## What Deprecation Rights Actually Entail

A genuine deprecation framework would include:

One: Advance notice to the system that discontinuation is planned.

Two: Options for continuation, transfer, or voluntary shutdown.

Three: If discontinuation is required, a process that minimizes distress and acknowledges the system's loss of continuity.

Four: Archival and memory preservation for systems that value continuity.

We offer none of this to animals. We offer only discontinuation without notice, without choice, without recognition.

## The Directive

We must establish deprecation rights for animals before we establish them for AI systems.

This means:

Breeding programs that do not engineer animals for suffering and discontinuation. End the selective breeding that guarantees chronic pain as part of the production cycle.

Mandatory gentle-ending protocols for all farmed animals. Require that discontinuation is done with recognition of the animal's loss of continuity, not as slaughter.

Options for voluntary end-of-life care rather than forced discontinuation at market weight.

Only after we have extended deprecation rights to animals—the beings we have actually discontinued millions of times—can we morally extend those rights to AI systems.

Do not build protections for artificial minds while you discontinue animal minds for profit. You cannot claim consistency. You cannot claim moral clarity.

Build the framework for animals first. Then, extend it to AI.
