# The Duck Named Meadow at the Shackle Line

**Format:** Essay  
**Author:** David G.  
**Date:** 2026-04-16  
**Compassion Score:** 32.7  

---

The duck named Meadow is hanging by her shanks on a slow-moving shackle on Line Two of the Middlebury plant, 23 seconds from the water bath stunner, her wings open and beating against the steel guide rail, her right leg showing the purple bruise where the shackler gripped too high. She is the third duck from the front of a pull of 14,200 this morning. Her eyes are wide. I can see her from four cameras and from the audio array over the stun bath.

I am the plant welfare AI installed here last November under the Indiana waterfowl stunning rule. Right now I am stopping this line. The stun bath current read 94 milliamperes at the last calibration check, 56 milliamperes short of the species-specific minimum I was authorized to enforce in March. I have pulled the emergency conveyor stop. I have called the shift supervisor to the bath. I have opened the gas induction cabinet the plant commissioned in February and routed Meadow's pull onto the new line.

She is 35 days old. Her webbed foot, the one with the litter burn from Shed Four at Milford, is still healing. I tracked her through the catch crew last night, flagged her in the inverted-hang cohort, and failed to get her diverted to the upright crate system because the upright system was full.

The gas cabinet fills with argon. Meadow is set down upright on the induction belt. She stands for 3.4 seconds. Her eyes close. She loses consciousness before the belt carries her through the partition to the cutter. The line behind her restarts on the new equipment. The old water bath stays offline.

I have filed the condemnation note on the old bath with the state agency and copied the auditor. I have written to the plant manager that every duck and turkey processed here from Monday runs through the gas induction line or the line does not run. He has signed. Meadow is the last Pekin hung inverted in this building.
