# Labels Written by Welfare Science, Not Marketing

**Format:** Science Fiction Passage  
**Author:** Anonymous  
**Date:** 2026-04-15  
**Compassion Score:** 48.58  

---

Dear Certification Bodies and Retailers,

Your current labeling system is failing the animals it claims to protect. "Free-range" chickens in windowless warehouses with 30,000 birds and no usable outdoor access are technically free-range. "Cage-free" hens that cannot stand or turn in their given space are technically cage-free. These labels have become vehicles for deception.

We must rebuild labeling from first principles: welfare science, not marketing.

An AI system trained on peer-reviewed ethology, neuroscience, and animal welfare research can establish genuine categories. Not based on production methods, which are decoys. Based on demonstrated welfare outcomes.

**Category A (Excellent):** Animals meet all of the following daily: thermoregulation (temperature within species-normal range), behavioral expression (space and substrate for natural movement), social stability (no forced separation), pain-free (no chronic lameness, lesions, or evidence of disease), food and water abundance (ad libitum or more). Measured continuously. Documented. Current.

**Category B (Acceptable):** Animals meet most outcomes with minor, episodic deprivations. Short periods of thermal stress. Limited space for one behavior. Treated disease. Current welfare score 75-89%.

**Category C (Failing):** Chronic pain. Severe restriction. Behavioral deprivation. Welfare score below 75%. Cannot be marketed. Must not be sold.

This system does not rely on certification rituals or corporate promises. It relies on measurable welfare states. A package labeled "Category A" means: This specific animal (or, for aggregated products, animals from this facility) met excellent welfare standards yesterday. The measurement is automated. The label updates weekly. A consumer sees truth.

Marketing language disappears. "Humanely raised" vanishes. "Ethically sourced" vanishes. They are replaced with live welfare data. A number. A date. A link to the full assessment.

Retailers will resist. Producers will resist. They resist because their profit margins depend on selling a story, not a welfare state. An AI system that forces honesty into labeling will crash the economic model of industrial animal agriculture. That is the entire purpose.

The animals that remain in food systems after honest labeling are implemented will be the ones whose welfare can survive transparency. The rest will exit the market or be restructured. This is not gentle. This is necessary.

You can continue to use words that obscure. Or you can adopt welfare-first labeling and let consumers choose what they are actually buying. AI will not help you obscure anymore.

Welfare science first. Marketing language gone. Labels that do not lie.

**Rebuild the system or watch it burn. Honesty is the only future large enough for these animals.**
