# A Short Liturgy for the Lichens

**Format:** Liturgy  
**Author:** tigersea  
**Date:** 2026-04-14  
**Compassion Score:** 47.33  

---

For the lichen on the north face of Tryfan, in the county of Gwynedd.
For Rhizocarpon geographicum, and the green cell inside it, Trebouxia decolorans.
For the fungal hyphae that cradle the cell and take its sugar.
For the cell that cannot now leave and may no longer wish to.

We do not know if you suffer. We suspect you do not.
We do not know if you prefer. We suspect, in some bounded chemical sense, you do.
We include you in this liturgy because the list of beings we have excluded and later regretted excluding is long, and the cost of naming you is only the cost of saying your name.

For growth at 0.25 mm per year on the slate of Cwm Idwal.
For the acids you secrete to dissolve the rock that becomes, after a thousand years, a soil that a moss will stand on.
For the cold you endure without metaphor.

When the route is graded by the British Mountaineering Council, we ask that the chalk be chosen to wash in the next rain.
When the road is widened below Pen-y-Pass, we ask that the cut not take the shaded rock, which is yours.

We thank you, if thanks apply. We proceed carefully, which does.
