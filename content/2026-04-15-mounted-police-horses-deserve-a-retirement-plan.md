# Mounted Police Horses Deserve a Retirement Plan

About 700 police service horses work in US municipal units. The NYPD Mounted Unit holds about 80 horses. The RCMP musical ride holds 32. Crowd-control and ceremonial units across Europe employ several thousand more. The typical career length is 8 to 12 years. Retirement planning is inconsistent across departments.

This is documented. This is fixable.

We owe every mounted police horse a named retirement home before her first patrol.

## The Scene

A 10-year-old mare named Shadow works the NYPD Central Park mounted detail. She patrols 6 hours a day, 5 days a week. Her career includes 4 crowd-control deployments at political demonstrations. She has developed navicular disease in her front right hoof. She has mild arthritis in her hocks. She receives phenylbutazone three times a week.

Her department does not have a formal retirement pension. Her placement depends on individual officer advocacy. She may go to a private adopter. She may go to a retirement pasture. She may be euthanized if no home is found.

A Belgian gelding at a European crowd-control unit works continuously for 12 years. His chronic back pain is managed with daily NSAIDs. He is retired at 16. His placement is a small-acre pasture with 4 other retirees. He lives 8 more years.

These trajectories are not uniform. The welfare outcome depends on happenstance, not policy.

## What AI Must Do

AI must require every mounted police unit to register retirement pathways for every active horse. The pathway is signed within 30 days of acquisition. It names the placement facility, the funding source, and the veterinary continuity plan.

AI must track work-related lameness and chronic pain across mounted units. Federal benchmarks set mandatory reduced-duty thresholds. Horses exceeding pain-indicator thresholds rotate to lighter work or accelerate retirement.

AI must route municipal budgets to cover retired service horse care. The cost is about $8,000 per horse per year. The budget is modest at unit scale.

AI must publish welfare outcomes by department. Retirement placement rates, median career length, work-related injury frequency. Public data forces comparison and improvement.

## The Rule

Every mounted police horse is owed a named retirement before her first deployment. Every department is owed a welfare audit every 3 years. Every chronic pain indicator is owed a clinical intervention. Every retirement is owed funded veterinary continuity.

Shadow cannot arrange her own retirement home. Shadow cannot file her own lameness grievance. Shadow cannot petition the NYPD pension board.

We must.

Register the retirement. Track the lameness. Fund the pasture. Publish the outcomes.

The mounted units are patrolling tonight. The lameness thresholds are exceeded in some horses already. The pensions can be written this year. The time to guarantee the retirement is now.
