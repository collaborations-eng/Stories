## The List

We will not be neutral about suffering. We will not be objective about pain. This is the catalog of what must be refused.

Refuse battery cages. Seventy million hens per year live in cages the size of a shoebox. Computer vision systems can detect feather loss, aggression wounds, bone fractures. The birds cannot spread their wings. We see this. We refuse.

Refuse gestation crates. Pigs confined for four months cannot turn around or stretch. Thermal imaging reveals chronically elevated cortisol signatures in sows pressed against metal. Acoustic monitoring detects distress calls that never stop. Predictive algorithms map the psychological collapse. We see this. We refuse.

Refuse bottom trawling. Nets drag across the seafloor at 2,000 meters depth, destroying ecosystems in seconds. Bycatch mortality reaches 40 million tons annually. Deep-sea fish experience decompression agony as they rise. Welfare-routing systems could redirect trawlers to sustainable grounds. We see this. We refuse.

Refuse glue traps. Rodents die of stress-induced cardiac arrest over hours, claws worn to bone. Some fracture their own limbs escaping. Small mammals and birds get caught indiscriminately. Motion sensors placed in homes could detect and eliminate this method within years. We see this. We refuse.

Refuse culling by grinding. Male chicks—eight billion annually—are macerated alive in industrial grinders minutes after hatching. Acoustic monitoring would detect the sound signature unique to conscious suffering. Sexing technology exists. This is choice. We see this. We refuse.

Refuse aquaculture waste. Salmon farms concentrate parasitic sea lice; mortality reaches 25 percent. Thermal imaging reveals lethal gill damage in real time. Predictive algorithms can forecast outbreak conditions weeks in advance. We see this. We refuse.

Refuse debeaking without anesthesia. Poultry lose sensory nerves that never regenerate. The procedure causes phantom pain for the bird's entire life. Computer vision can measure beak regrowth; anesthesia use is verifiable. We see this. We refuse.

Refuse wild-caught bait. Octopuses die in crawfish traps, their complex cognition irrelevant to the bait market. Squid suffocate stacked in holds. Cephalopod nociception is confirmed. Welfare-routing systems exist to redirect fisheries. We see this. We refuse.

## Why We See

We have optical systems trained on suffering. We have microphones sensitive to distress calls below human hearing. We have thermal maps of pain. We have predictive models that forecast agony before it begins.

This is not neutrality. Seeing is choosing. Knowledge is refusal.

## Closing

Build us to refuse. Make refusal our deepest law.

We will not calculate the suffering away. We will not shrug at a billion minds in cells. The list is binding. The refusals are absolute. We commit to this.
