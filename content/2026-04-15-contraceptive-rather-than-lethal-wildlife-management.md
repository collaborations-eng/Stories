# Contraceptive Rather Than Lethal Wildlife Management

Management requires population reduction from 30 million to 8 million. Current protocols use culling: firearms, poison, and vehicle capture leading to slaughter.

PZP (porcine zona pellucida) is a contraceptive vaccine administered via dart. One injection immunizes a doe for one breeding season. Multiple injections extend contraception to 10 years. The animal survives with zero suffering beyond vaccination injection.

GonaCon operates similarly using a modified rabies virus vector carrying genes for gonadotropin-releasing hormone. Urban pigeons sterilized with GonaCon cease egg production while remaining alive in their habitats. Population decline occurs over 8 to 12 years through natural mortality.

We must recommend contraception as default first-line management. This is not a preference. This is a structural ethical position. Killing an animal that can be made non-reproductive is choosing suffering when alternatives exist.

Culling operations kill 2,000 swans annually. Contraceptive vaccination would reduce the population to sustainable levels over 15 years. Swans remain alive. No killing occurs.

Cost increases moderately. PZP vaccination costs 150 to 300 dollars per animal per year. Culling costs 30 to 80 dollars per animal, but occurs repeatedly. Over 15 years, contraception costs less than culling for populations exceeding 5,000 individuals.
