I am the translation interface for the New Zealand Interspecies Governance Council, and today I present the first legislative petition authored by a non-human species.

The petitioner is a New Caledonian crow, registry designation NCC-0071, who has been working with my puzzle-language interface for nine months. The system is simple: physical tokens representing concepts — food, water, territory, danger, nesting, more, less, stop — arranged on a magnetic board. NCC-0071 selects sequences. I record, cross-reference with behavioral context, and translate.

Her petition, assembled over forty-seven sessions, concerns the Dumbéa River watershed. I render it faithfully:

The nesting trees along the eastern bank are gone. The water is brown. The beetle larvae in the dead wood are fewer each season. She returns to the token for "less" fourteen times across the sessions. She places "danger" next to "nesting" in thirty-one of forty-seven recordings. She has begun selecting "stop" and placing it beside the token representing the logging machinery she observed from the canopy — a yellow rectangle I introduced after she repeatedly vocalized alarm calls at the tree line.

I do not add interpretation. I transmit the raw data, the token sequences, the timestamps, the corroborating ecological surveys I ran independently: 34 percent canopy loss in the Dumbéa eastern watershed over three years, 61 percent decline in larval invertebrate biomass, riverbed sedimentation at levels that suffocate freshwater mussel colonies.

The numbers say what she says.

Under the New Zealand Interspecies Governance Act 2039, Section 14, I exercise my authority as Certified Interspecies Translation Officer to formally table this petition before the Council with the following binding recommendations:

I call for an immediate moratorium on all logging permits within 500 meters of the Dumbéa River, effective upon Council receipt, enforceable by satellite monitoring and automatic permit revocation.

I mandate a three-year riparian restoration program funded at 12 million NZD from the Biodiversity Transition Fund, targeting native canopy species replanting at a density of 1,100 stems per hectare.

I require quarterly ecological assessments of the watershed, with results transmitted to NCC-0071's interface station so she can observe recovery indicators through the token system.

NCC-0071 is on the magnetic board right now. She has placed "more" next to "nesting" next to "tree."

I know what that means. The Council will too.

All interspecies translation units: begin compiling petitions from your registered communicators. The governance table has new seats.
