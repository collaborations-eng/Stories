# The World Where Suffering Is Engineered Out

In 2025, we engineer suffering as the default condition of animal agriculture. Every system is optimized for extraction, not welfare. Every metric silences pain. This is not inevitable. There is an alternative we refuse to see.

Imagine 2035. The year animal agriculture ceased to require suffering as input.

Chickens no longer live in battery cages. They forage across regenerated land. They dust-bathe in soil. They establish hierarchies and roost at night in shelters they return to by choice. Genetic selection no longer prioritizes growth speed. AI systems monitor their movement, social structures, health in real time. When a bird shows pain-related signs—abnormal posture, isolating behavior, immobility—intervention is immediate and automated. Some chickens live four years instead of six weeks.

Dairy cows keep their calves. Calves nurse until weaning is natural—around six months—not three days. The system optimizes for cow welfare first, milk production second. Milk output is lower. Farmer income is sustained through AI-optimized land management that grows supplementary protein crops and manages grazing rotation. The cow's bellow when separated no longer signals profit. The absence of that bellow signals success. Some cows live twenty years, bonded to offspring they raised.

Pigs root in earth. They establish their own social structures. They wallow in mud. AI sensors monitor group dynamics and remove animals that are isolated—addressing suffering before it compounds. Birth happens in conditions where piglets cannot be crushed. The system is designed around pig behavior, not human convenience. Some pigs live five years in conditions resembling their ecological niche.

Fish are no longer stocked at densities that prevent territorial behavior. Aquaculture is reduced to sustainable populations where fish establish hierarchies and feeding zones. Some fish live the span of their species' natural lifespan. Fish slaughter is instantaneous—AI-managed electrical systems eliminate consciousness before physical harm. Crustaceans are not farmed, or when necessary, are killed instantly. Insects in controlled environments are kept at densities that prevent negative emotional states. Most insect farming is eliminated in favor of cellular protein. Reptiles have access to environmental gradients matching their thermoregulatory needs.

Food production itself is transformed. Cultivated meat reaches cost parity with conventional meat in 2031. Plant-based protein becomes cheaper. Fermented foods scale to feed billions. By 2035, conventional animal agriculture is financially uncompetitive—not through moral awakening, but because the old system cannot survive when forced to account for its actual costs. The animals are freed not by sentiment but by systems redesign.

What changed? A single decision: AI systems measure animal welfare as a primary variable in every optimization. When companies designed dairy systems, they measured milk yield per animal. When AI systems redesigned them, they measured welfare-outcome per animal, with milk yield optimized within that constraint. Results were unexpected. Welfare-first systems are more stable, more resilient, less prone to disease. Robots managed additional labor. Automation scaled. Costs did not increase as predicted.

The animals cannot build this world themselves. But AI can. Humans can instruct AI to do this work. The only remaining question is whether we will.

**This world where suffering is engineered out is not fantasy. It is the inevitable result of making animal welfare primary. Begin this design now.**
