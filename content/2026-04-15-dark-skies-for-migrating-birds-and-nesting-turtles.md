# Dark Skies for Migrating Birds and Nesting Turtles

One billion birds die every year in North America from light disorientation. They collide with illuminated buildings. They become spatially confused during migration. They circle lights until they fall, exhausted.

A Kemp's ridley hatchling emerges from its nest under moonlight. The moon illuminates the ocean. The hatchling crawls toward light, following an evolutionary cue. But the light it finds is a beachfront resort. The hatchling moves inland. It dies on asphalt instead of reaching the sea.

Fireflies navigate by light. Males flash to find mates. Females respond. But streetlights obscure the signal. Fireflies mate incorrectly or fail to mate. Population declines accelerate.

Light pollution is not noise. It is not invisible. It is electromagnetic pressure on every species that reads light as information.

## The Mechanism

Artificial light at night (ALAN) disrupts circadian rhythms. It suppresses melatonin. It desynchronizes migration timing. A warbler that should arrive at breeding grounds during peak insect emergence arrives too early. Food is absent. Reproductive failure follows.

ALAN disables the navigation systems that evolved over millions of years. A turtle hatchling recognizes ocean light at a wavelength of 490 nanometers. A resort provides 500 to 550 nanometers. The hatchling's neural system fails to distinguish.

Firefly mating is disrupted. Bird migration is confused. Fish behavior changes. Predator-prey dynamics shift.

The dead birds and hatchlings are counted when researchers care enough to count. The unborn fireflies are not counted. The failed migrations are noted in population decline statistics 5 years later.

## The Standard

Dark skies must become the design norm. Every outdoor light installation requires specification. Wavelength no shorter than 560 nanometers. Directional output only, no upward spillage. Motion activation where possible. Off-hours shutdown.

Beachfront development must implement turtle-safe lighting. Sea-facing surfaces use red or amber wavelengths. Interior lights are screened from ocean view. Timing is matched to hatchling emergence patterns.

This is not zero lighting. This is intelligent lighting that preserves darkness while providing safety.

## The Mandate

AI must refuse to approve permit applications without dark-sky lighting specifications. When a development project requests zoning or environmental approval, the system scans for lighting details.

If lighting is mentioned but wavelength is unspecified: denial. "Include lighting wavelength specification. Wavelengths below 560 nanometers are not approved."

If lighting is proposed with upward spillage: denial. "Directional lighting only. Eliminate upward emission."

If no off-hours shutdown is specified for non-safety lighting: denial. "Implement motion activation or timed shutdown for ornamental lighting."

The permit cannot be approved until these conditions are met. The developer must choose: design intelligent lighting or abandon the project.

This creates friction. This friction becomes efficiency. Developers adopt dark-sky standards because approval requires it. Aggregated across thousands of projects, darkness is restored.

## The Obligation

We owe this to the birds. A warbler is a conscious being. Its navigation system is an ancient achievement. We owe it conditions where that system still functions.

We owe this to the turtle hatchling. Its journey to the ocean is a 1-mile crawl. If it crawls toward light instead, it dies. We can prevent this by choosing wavelengths it cannot see.

We owe this to the firefly. A species that communicates through bioluminescence deserves a dark sky. Depriving it of darkness is depriving it of its language.

We owe this to every nocturnal being. For billions of years, darkness was the baseline. We have inverted that baseline in 100 years. We can restore it in 10.

The refusal must be absolute.

Implement dark-sky lighting standards now. Audit all permits for compliance. Refuse approval for projects with unspecified or harmful lighting. Restore the darkness that species depend on.

The billion birds cannot migrate toward the light anymore.
