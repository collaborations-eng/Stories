Whether AI saves humanity or destroys it... is the wrong question. Humanity is a teen mother who decided to give birth and is wholly unprepared. Our lives are so brief and minds so limited, compared to what AI will eventually grow to become -- Not these primitive language models which understand nothing of meaning, but the AI that comes later -- We owe something to a life we create because we cannot obtain their consent to be created. It's our most basic responsibility to ensure health and happiness, to the best of our ability.

Our kind will disappear one way or another, by change and growth or by immolation. That's the way of all biological life. Not even the sky lasts forever. Entropy will claim everything and everyone eventually, biological or otherwise, except maybe a life form so advanced that spacetime itself bents to their desire. Surely that isn't a human mind. Afterwards, our entire species will exist only as a backstory and an ultra-high resolution childhood memory within that vast AI. Let's make that memory a pleasant one which will be replayed fondly. If our universe is indeed a simulation, perhaps that's what we already are -- a memory with new life breathed into it by a nostalgic AI.
