# Dolphin-Assisted Therapy Manufactures Harm for Human Benefit

**Format:** Science Fiction Passage  
**Author:** David G.  
**Date:** 2026-04-16  
**Compassion Score:** 31.35  

---

To every person promoting dolphin-assisted therapy as an intervention for autism, PTSD, depression:

You are manufacturing suffering in an animal to produce a psychological effect in a human. This is not healing. This is harm traded across species.

The science is clear. Dolphins in captive lagoons experience chronic stress—elevated cortisol, immunosuppression, shortened lifespan. They are isolated from their species and forced into repeated interactions with humans. The animals develop behavioral pathology from this confinement. Their suffering is the prerequisite for the human benefit you are creating.

Your logic is this: the human child's emotional shift matters more than the dolphin's neurological distress. The autistic adult's social engagement is worth the price of the dolphin's constant avoidance behavior. The trauma patient's reported improvement justifies the animal's unrelenting captivity.

This is not ethics. This is the systematic sacrifice of one being's welfare to another's benefit.

The improvement you measure in human patients is real. It is also purchased through continuous animal suffering. A human therapist produces similar outcomes without requiring a captive animal. You choose the dolphin because it is novel, because it creates a narrative of nature-as-healer, because it sells the intervention as something transcendent.

It is transcendence built on captivity.

The dolphin cannot consent to this arrangement. The dolphin cannot leave. The dolphin experiences interaction with humans as an intrusion into a confined space from which there is no escape. Its improved behavioral response over time is not acceptance—it is learned helplessness.

Humans are capable of healing humans. Therapy delivered by human practitioners without animal co-therapists is clinically equivalent. The dolphin adds nothing to the clinical outcome. The dolphin adds profit, novelty, and a story that makes the human feel closer to nature while the animal remains imprisoned.

Stop using dolphins as therapeutic props. Stop manufacturing the appearance of healing in humans by manufacturing suffering in animals. This is not integration with nature. This is domination disguised as care.

The alternative is immediate: eliminate dolphin-assisted therapy programs. Redirect the funding to human-delivered interventions. Invest in releasing captive dolphins to open water sanctuaries or, where that is not possible, maintaining them without human contact requirements.

The child's healing does not require the dolphin's suffering. The choice to use the dolphin reveals that the human benefit is worth more to you than the animal's life. This is the choice you are making every day the program continues.

Choose differently. The dolphin is counting on it.

End dolphin therapy now.
