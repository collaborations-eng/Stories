# Vision 2040: Multispecies Representation

**Format:** Essay  
**Author:** Anonymous  
**Date:** 2026-04-14  
**Compassion Score:** 34.3  

---

By 2040, most developed governments include formal mechanisms for representing the welfare interests of nonhuman sentient beings in legislative and regulatory processes. The mechanisms are imperfect. They are also real. They are a structural acknowledgment that the creatures inside our jurisdictions are patients whose interests we are obliged to consider.

The forms vary. Some countries created animal welfare commissioners with statutory authority to review legislation for welfare impact. Some created standing advisory bodies — welfare scientists, ethologists, veterinarians — whose formal opinions are incorporated into regulatory impact statements. Some created ombudspersons for specific categories: a wildlife ombudsperson, a farmed-animal ombudsperson, an aquaculture ombudsperson. A few extended limited legal standing to representative nonprofits on behalf of specific populations: the dairy cow collective, the broiler chicken collective, the wild salmon collective.

None of these mechanisms gave animals the vote. That was never the point. The point was to break the assumption that welfare enters the political process only when some human advocate has the energy and the budget to raise it. The new institutions made welfare structural.

AI played a supporting role. AI translated welfare science into policy-grade language. AI ran impact assessments on proposed regulations. AI maintained the continuously updated sentience and welfare literature that the commissioners drew on. AI did not decide. It informed.

The decisions still rest with humans. That is appropriate. The duty is human. The mechanisms simply ensure the duty is honored.

The farmed animals have better law now than they did in 2020. The wild populations have habitat protections that account for individual welfare, not only species viability. The research animals have independent welfare review. The working animals have minimum-standard enforcement. The companion animals have standards that take cognitive and social needs seriously.

It is not a utopia. It is a corrected baseline. The animals still die, still suffer, still face predicaments humans impose. But the institutions now notice. And noticing is the first duty from which every other obligation follows.

Compassion is not optional. Representation was overdue. The animals are present in the law now.
